General Overview

The triceratops, according to Scanella (2025), was a large, herbivorous (plant-eating), and quadrupedal reptile that lived during the late cretacious period. The triceratops featured a frill which had three horns, which gave rise to it's name (with "tri" meaning three, "cerat" meaning horned, and "ops" meaning face). The triceratops was also stated to have a body length of about 9 meters (30 feet), with the largest having a possible weight of 5,450-7,260 kg.

Physical Feats

The triceratops often used it's horns and frill to defend itself from oncoming predators. According to an article written by the BBC (2025), paleontologists in 1997 had uncovered a triceratops fossil that appeared to have its horn bitten by what seemed to be a Tyrannosaurus Rex. The fossil that was found also seemed to have healed, implying the triceratops to have survived encounters with the T-Rex (and possibly other predatory dinosaurs).

Discovery

The triceratops was discovered in 1887 by American scientist Othniel Charles Marsh in 1887. Initially, Marsh thought the horns of the triceratops (which was the first part of the dinosaur he found) to belong to a giant bison. Due to this misconception, Marsh gave the cerapod the name "Bison alticornis", meaning "Bison with big horns". Afterwards, Marsh uncovered more of the fossil, and realized that what he found was not a bison, rather, it was something else. Marsh, with this new discovery, then decided to name the fossil "triceratops".
